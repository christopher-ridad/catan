# Catan Game Rules

Source: *Catan Game Rules & Almanac*, 5th Edition (2015), Klaus Teuber / Catan GmbH & Mayfair Games, Inc.

---

## Game Components

- 19 terrain hexes (tiles)
- 6 sea frame pieces
- 9 harbor pieces
- 18 circular number tokens (numbered 2–12, no 7; only one 2 and one 12)
- 95 resource cards (Brick, Grain, Lumber, Ore, Wool)
- 25 development cards (14 Knight, 6 Progress, 5 Victory Point)
- 4 Building Costs cards
- 2 special cards: "Longest Road" and "Largest Army"
- 16 cities (4 per color)
- 20 settlements (5 per color)
- 60 roads (15 per color)
- 2 dice (1 yellow, 1 red)
- 1 robber

---

## Terrain Types & Resources

| Terrain     | Resource Produced |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Hills       | Brick             |
| Forest      | Lumber            |
| Mountains   | Ore               |
| Fields      | Grain             |
| Pasture     | Wool              |
| Desert      | Nothing           |

## Board Setup

### Fixed Beginner Setup
Lay out the 19 terrain hexes and number tokens exactly as shown in the beginner's map (Illustration A in the rulebook).

### Variable Setup (Experienced)
1. Shuffle terrain hexes face down, then place them face up randomly inside the frame in the standard 19-hex arrangement (3-4-5-4-3 rows).
2. Randomly place 1 harbor piece on each of the 9 harbor slots on the frame.
3. Place number tokens alphabetically (A–R) counterclockwise from a corner toward the center, skipping the Desert. Red numbers (6, 8) must not be adjacent to each other.
4. Place the robber on the Desert hex.

---

## Setting Up the Game

1. Each player selects a color and takes 5 settlements, 4 cities, 15 roads, and 1 Building Costs card.
2. Sort resource cards into 5 face-up stacks beside the board.
3. Shuffle development cards and place them face down beside the board.
4. Place the 2 special cards and dice beside the board.
5. Place the robber on the Desert.
6. Proceed to the **Setup Phase**.

---

## Setup Phase (Initial Placement)

The setup phase has **2 rounds**. Each player places 1 settlement and 1 adjacent road per round.

### Round 1 (Clockwise)
- Each player rolls both dice; the highest roller is the starting player.
- Starting player places 1 settlement on any unoccupied intersection, then 1 road adjacent to it.
- Other players follow **clockwise**.
- The Distance Rule applies: no settlement may be placed within 2 intersections of any existing settlement.

### Round 2 (Counter-clockwise)
- The player who went **last** in Round 1 goes **first** in Round 2.
- Players follow **counter-clockwise**, so the Round 1 starting player places their second settlement **last**.
- Each player places 1 settlement and 1 adjacent road (road must attach to the second settlement).
- **Immediately after placing the second settlement**, each player receives 1 resource card for each terrain hex adjacent to that settlement.

After Round 2, the player who placed last (the Round 1 starting player) begins the game by rolling for resource production.

> **Note (3-player game):** Nobody plays the red position on the beginner map.

---

## Turn Structure

Each turn has 3 phases in order:

### 1. Resource Production (mandatory)
- Roll both dice.
- Every player with a settlement or city adjacent to a hex matching the rolled number receives resources:
  - Settlement → 1 resource card per adjacent producing hex
  - City → 2 resource cards per adjacent producing hex
- If there are not enough resource cards in the bank to fulfill all production, **no one** receives that resource that turn (unless only 1 player is affected — that player gets as many as remain).

### 2. Trade (optional)
**Domestic Trade:** You may trade resource cards with any other player. Only the active player may be a party to trades; other players cannot trade among themselves.

**Maritime Trade:**
- Always available at **4:1** (4 identical resources → 1 of any resource).
- Settlement/city at a **Generic Harbor (3:1)** → trade 3 identical resources for 1 of any.
- Settlement/city at a **Special Harbor (2:1)** → trade 2 of that specific resource for 1 of any.

You may not give away cards, trade development cards, or trade matching resources.

### 3. Build (optional)
Pay resource cards and place pieces. You may build as many items as you can afford in one turn.

| Item              | Cost                          | Notes                                        |
|-------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Road              | 1 Brick + 1 Lumber            | Must connect to own road, settlement, or city; 1 per path |
| Settlement        | 1 Brick + 1 Lumber + 1 Wool + 1 Grain | Must connect to own road; Distance Rule applies |
| City              | 3 Ore + 2 Grain               | Replaces an existing settlement; worth 2 VP  |
| Development Card  | 1 Ore + 1 Wool + 1 Grain      | Draw top card; keep hidden                   |

**Supply limits:** Max 5 settlements, 4 cities, 15 roads per player.

## Placement Rules

### Distance Rule
A settlement may only be placed at an intersection where **all 3 adjacent intersections are vacant** (no settlement or city of any player). This applies during setup and normal play.

### Road Rules
- Roads are built on **paths** (edges between hexes or between a hex and the frame).
- Only 1 road per path.
- A new road must connect to the player's existing road, settlement, or city.
- Roads can be built along the coast.

---

## Special Cases

### Rolling a 7 / Robber
1. No resources are produced.
2. Any player holding **more than 7 resource cards** must discard half (rounded down).
3. The active player **must** move the robber to any other terrain hex (or the Desert).
4. The active player steals 1 random resource card from a player with a settlement or city adjacent to the robber's new hex. If multiple players qualify, the active player chooses.
5. While the robber occupies a hex, that hex produces **no resources** when its number is rolled.

### Development Cards
- You may play **1 development card per turn** (not the same turn it was purchased), at any time during your turn.
- Exception: Victory Point cards may be revealed on the turn purchased if they give you 10+ VP for the win.

#### Knight Card (Purple)
- Move the robber (same rules as rolling a 7, steps 3–4).
- First player to have **3 knight cards** face-up takes the "Largest Army" special card (2 VP). Another player with more knights takes it.

#### Progress Cards (Green)
- **Road Building:** Place 2 free roads immediately (normal placement rules apply).
- **Year of Plenty:** Take any 2 resource cards from the supply; may use them immediately.
- **Monopoly:** Name 1 resource type; all other players give you all of that resource from their hands.

#### Victory Point Cards (Orange)
- Worth 1 VP each. Keep hidden until declaring victory (or game end).

---

## Special Cards

### Longest Road
- Awarded to the first player who builds a **continuous road of at least 5 segments** (branches don't count).
- Worth **2 VP**. If another player builds a longer road, they take the card immediately.
- Special case: if the longest road is broken by an opponent's settlement, the card may be set aside if there is a tie or no qualifying road remains.

### Largest Army
- Awarded to the first player to have **3 knight cards** played face-up.
- Worth **2 VP**. Taken immediately by any player who surpasses the current holder's knight count.

## Victory Points

| Source                  | VP Value |
|-------------------------|----------|
| Settlement              | 1        |
| City                    | 2        |
| Longest Road card       | 2        |
| Largest Army card       | 2        |
| Victory Point dev. card | 1 each   |

Each player starts with **2 settlements = 2 VP**.

**Win condition:** First player to reach **10 VP on their own turn** wins immediately.

---

## Key Definitions

- **Intersection:** A point where 3 hexes meet (or 1–2 hexes meet the frame). Settlements and cities are placed here.
- **Path:** An edge shared by 2 hexes or a hex and the frame. Roads are placed here.
- **Harbor:** A coastal location allowing favorable maritime trade (3:1 generic or 2:1 specific resource).
- **Desert:** The one terrain hex that produces no resources. The robber starts here.
